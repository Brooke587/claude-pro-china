## 1. Why Claude Is Hard to Access from China

Before anything else, it helps to understand what is actually blocking you. There are four separate friction points, and each one needs its own fix:

1. **Geographic restriction.** Anthropic's terms of service do not list mainland China as a supported region. Requests from Chinese IP addresses are typically rejected or redirected. Solution: a stable overseas connection (VPN / proxy) that does not constantly rotate IPs.
2. **Phone verification.** Claude requires SMS verification during signup. The `+86` prefix is not accepted for new registrations. Solution: a foreign virtual mobile number from a reputable SMS-receiving service — this is where 1001SMS comes in.
3. **Payment.** Claude Pro costs USD 20 per month and is billed through international card processors. Chinese UnionPay and most domestic debit cards are declined. Solution: either a working virtual card, a pre-paid foreign card, or a third-party top-up / shared-plan service.
4. **Account stability.** Even after signup, accounts created from China frequently get suspended if the IP keeps changing, the fingerprint is suspicious, or the payment is reversed. Solution: consistent IP, consistent browser, clean Gmail, no shared logins.

The rest of this guide walks through each fix in order.

## 5. Avoiding Claude Account Bans in China

Getting the account is only half the battle. Keeping it alive is the other half. Accounts registered from China are banned more often than any other cohort, and it is almost always due to a handful of predictable mistakes:

- **Rotating IPs.** Logging in from three countries in one week is a red flag. Pick one exit country and stay on it.
- **Shared logins.** Sharing your Claude login with friends usually triggers a ban within a week.
- **Chargebacks or declined cards.** A failed payment or chargeback is a near-instant ban.
- **Abusive prompts.** Violating Anthropic's usage policy (CSAM, weapons of mass destruction, targeted harassment, etc.) gets accounts permanently disabled and the underlying payment method blacklisted.
- **Fingerprint mismatch.** Using ten different browsers with aggressive anti-detect profiles can flag the account. A single clean browser profile is safer than a fortress of spoofed ones.

Best practice: **one VPN location, one browser, one Gmail, one payment method, one human user.**
